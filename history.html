Yearly Recaps
Preamble
Well we just started our 20+th year of the NJCO Fantasy Football league (you start losing count after a certain period)! Yes, it has had different names in the past, and members have come and gone, but it still has had some core elements. I thought it would be fun to take a look back at the previous 20+ years, the drama, the rule changes, and just the evolution of the game that we enjoy so much today. For you relatively new guys, it's also a chance to see why things are done in certain ways as well. Enjoy!

Pre-Story is I was introduced to the world of Fantasy Sports through a summer visit from my good ol' friend Matt Hayduk in 1996. The website was SmallWorld Sports, and he taught me about Fantasy Baseball and I was hooked right away. During that fall, I attempted to start my own Fantasy Football league at my middle-school lunch table. (I still had the notebook of the first draft as of a couple of years ago :) It was an utter disaster though as manually calculating stats was just too time consuming. My friend Jay Tsui though had introduced me to Sandbox.com and their fantasy football. I was in a league with him and his friends for two years, with in which was fun but I thought could be better.
1999-2002
(The Sandbox Years)
I found out how to run a league at Sandbox and asked a few of my friends if they wanted in... this included Matt, Dan Morales, TJ Smith, Dave Parenti, and Jay Tsui. I distinctly remember Jay bragging about how he got Terrell Davis, Randy Moss, and Steve Young in the draft and that his team was "GOD LIKE!". Davis blew out his knee, Young only played three games, and Moss had a slight sophomore slump. Jay would leave the league following that year. The next few years the games were played using Sandbox.com's scoring system, which included individual defensive players. Others joined the league, along the likes of Tony, Big Matt, and Jeff I believe. Drafts would consistently see guys like Derrick Brooks and Ray Lewis go in the top three rounds- it was fun. Logos were introduced and when stats came out on Monday Morning, most of us would race to the computer to see who had won. I can't remember who was Champions during this time and Sandbox is no longer around for research- but this was when the league really started to take off. The league switched from points only to head-to-head and did lose members TJ Smith, Dave Parenti, Matt Egan, and a small handful of others due to lack of participation.
2003
(First Season at Yahoo!)
Sandbox, in it's infinite wisdom, decided to start to try and squeeze money out of it's members during the 2002 season. All regular features that used to be free started to cost money except for the league. I started to look for free options and it came down between CBSsportsline.com and Yahoo. I did a test run of leagues in Yahoo during 2002 and loved their layout... Sandbox went full-pay in 2003 and the league jumped ship to Yahoo! Pete, Luke, Naveen Swamy, and Big Al/Travis all joined the league. Individual defensive players were replaced with team defenses. Jeff romped through this season going 15-2 setting an all-time record for wins that can never be beat due to future changes was technically matched by SOB in 2023's "cruise control" season. Best team name: Sneaky Fire Spitters (Pete)
2004
(Huge Changes Year)
This year was the first year of doing playoffs, actually having a prize pool (in which the rule of "if you go undefeated you get ALL OF THE MONEY" was established - and still stands), and was by far the most hotly debated ending to the season. Most of us were working at Elephant Wireless that summer leading into the season, and trash talk started early. Tom Leon joined the league as well as Ian, who's entry was hotly debated. In order to keep even teams, Naveen Swamy was removed from the league as there was overwhelming support to keep the league more Edison focused and Luke didn't sign up. The end of the season saw much controversy as Big Matt had dropped marquee names in which Lil Matt was trying to pick up for the playoffs. This came with much debate over the can't cut list and some locking of teams. Ian had also made a splash right away, making it all the way to the Championship game in his first year. His run ended as I took the first ever playoff championship after squeaking by on a tie-break win vs Lil Matt in the first round due to an Alge Crumpler fumble. This game was the reason fractional points were introduced.

Best team name: The PORKSODA POKERS (Tom L)
2005
(The Last Expansion and KEEPERS!!)
This season saw the league expand to the full 12-teams that you see now. The addition of Chris aka "Eggy" and the re-entry of TJ Smith brought the league up to that number and a new "Keeper Rule" was approved by the league (two). The league also did a draft-lottery for the first time as we knew the 12-team limit was set in stone now- this would curb anybody from intentionally tanking the end of the year. The draft was probably the most talked about and anticipated of the league's history as it was the last one with complete free reign of players to pick from. As the league went on, this was also the first year of locking playoff teams from adding (after the debacle at the end of 2004). Big Matt took home his first championship in a close championship over myself as TJ Duckett rode the bench for me for some reason.

Best Team Name: CockItToTheRight
2006
(Changing of Times)
2006 was a sad year as we saw the passing of an original Lil Matt Hayduk. His brother Luke took over his team and the league was changed forever. We tried to spruce up the draft by doing a live in-person draft for the first time at Nan's house- this was the only time we'd ever try this. The smack talk was still around and rivalries still existed, but something has lacked in the league since. A four-way tie for 3rd place forced two 8-6 teams to miss the playoffs, and the other two Big Matt and Jeff finished #1/#2 after all was said and done. Rule changes were proposed- such as a salary cap structure w/ auction draft and unlocking of playoff teams, but no rules were actually changed.

Best Team Name: Chicken's Crusaders
2007
(BFB Wuh Oh!)
The 2007 season had a raise in the entry fee (from $20 to $30) and a failed pursuit of a 3-peat by Big Matt, but will probably be best known for the heated trash talk for that year's Black Friday Bowl. Multiple posts went back and forth about who should be captains, ending gloriously with myself and Tony shutting out Jeff's team that Black Friday. As for the fantasy side of things, Jeff did get his revenge as he and his Tom Brady led team won the championship over Dan... my own team, despite being far superior in the regular season, finished out of the money. Best Team Name:

Real Men of Genius
2008
(You Traded Who?!?)
This season saw the removal of Eggy and the welcomed addition of Greg into the league. The season itself was pretty typical, with no changes made, but was highlighted by two of the biggest trades to ever go down in the league. I had traded not only Chris Johnson/Tony Gonzalez for Gates/Roddy White but also Adrian Peterson for Tomlinson straight up. The moves helped me make the championship game but only to be denied a title by Big Matt. Best Team Name:

Randy Marsh... nuff said.
2009
(Start of the Non-Kicker Era)
The playoffs from 2008 had come down to a kicker on Monday Night between Jeff and I. Watching the game at Tony's house, we both looked at each other and went... welp, it's a complete crap shoot huh? We then decided shortly after to remove kickers from the league as they are probably the most unpredictable asset of any fantasy team, regardless of sport. Anthony Rispoli was welcomed into the league to replace an inactive TJ Smith this year as well. Ian claimed the title after winning the first round with the smallest margin of victory ever in a playoff game (fractional point era). The record book was introduced and still has some records that stand present day (and probably won't ever be beat- like Pete's high score *This was eventually topped in 2019).

Best Team Name: S Lyle's True Hollywood Story
2010
(Unexpected Tragedy)
2010 was probably the year in which the league was most shaken up in devastating ways. The league started off fantastically with a draft occurring at the Rispoli house. This was one of the first times in years we had the majority of the league together on draft day. During the season though we had the unexpected death of Anthony Rispoli, in which Pete would take over his team... to this day, he has been the only manager approved of managing two teams at once. Just a couple of weeks after though, Pete had also unexpectedly passed away. The league was very somber during the championship weeks, Tony had claimed his first ever title in the end.

Best Team Name: All Day Jackin'
2011
(Ch-ch-ch-changes)
With what had occurred the previous year, the league had to add two replacement members. Tommy Cohen and Chris Martin made the cut. With the addition of two new members, other changes had been proposed that made fantastic sense. The trading of draft picks brought about a whole new way of trading... waivers from first-game thru Tuesday brought about a level playing field instead of rewarding those with the computer closest to them... and the creation of conferences and rivalries brought a whole new twist to how the game is played. The 12-team league had been split into two conferences, mainly determined by the rivalries that had developed over the years. Teams would play all of their conference foes twice, three out of conference games, and their rival- not once, not twice, but three times. The conferences themselves were named in remembrance of our dear friends Matt Hayduk and Pete and Anthony Rispoli. The season, at least to me, brought about a new level of energy not felt since the '05 season. Chris Martin took home the 'ship in his first year, overcoming an 8-6 regular season. Maybe he had a little help from up above?

Best Team Name: I cut my Bush
2012
(Mini-Dynasty 1.0)
Mr. Greg Crawford came, saw, and conquered in the year 2012. Our entry fee was upped to $100, so this maybe gave Greg the motivation to finally put it all together. A draft which saw Tony draft Michael Turner in the first round (Michael Turner???) then saw a dominant 11-win season put together by Jeff. He faced off against Rispoli Champ Team Crawford in an epic championship match while Travis, who's team put up a measly 52.57 points in the first round playoff loss, claimed third place with a monster 135 point consolation game.

Best Team Name: HabitualLineSteppers
2013
(Mini-Dynasty book-end)
Mr. Greg Crawford came, saw, and conquered AGAIN in the year 2013. The Rispoli Conference absolutely destroyed the Hayduk when out-of-conference weeks came up and then went on to prove it in the playoffs as both Sean and Greg made the championship game. In what was only decided by 7 points, Greg claimed the first back-to-back championships since Big Matt did the trick back in 05-06! Guess who was the first pick in the draft... Trent... Richardson. Ewwww, clean up on Aisle 1!

Best Team Name: Shady Rodgers Asylum
2014
(The year of the Underdog)
Fantasy football doesn't always work out perfect, some weeks some teams just get lucky. 2014 proved this big time as Tony's record-setting team, who was stacked, somehow choked away a first round playoff matchup to a team he had nearly 400 more points on during the season! Then, on the other matchup, Jeff put up a whopping 57 points... and WON BY DOUBLE DIGITS over a 10-win Team Crawford looking for the three-peat. Jeff then went on to claim another title for the "Big 3" over Sean Kenney while Tony put up a score for third that would have easily won him the championship. Price is right horns!

Best Team Name: The Juice Box Guy
2015
(The Collapse)
We saw a new member, Jack, come in to replace Zack's dormant team in 2015. The era of "Goldust" was born. SOB's team started off the season 9-0 and he was staring at possibly being the first to ever finish undefeated and take home ALL THE MONEY! All of this AFTER keeping Justin Forsett!?!? However, one loss came in week 10, then another in 11, and another in 13... and before you know it he was losing in the first round of the playoffs! Tom Leon rode a wave the last quarter of the season, reeling off 5 straight wins to claim the NJCO title!

Best Team Name: There All Is Aching
2016
(Redemption!)
After the tragic 2015 season, SOB was champing at the bit to get back the next season. In a hotley contested Rispoli, in which four teams all went over 1230 pts, Teamy McTeamface wound up ripping off his own 5 straight wins to finally win the whole damn thing again after multiple near miss 2nd place finishes since his last title way back in '04. During this season though we ended up saying goodbye though to two long-time members (one original) as things just didn't work out. But, this would spark a whoooole new ballgame going forward!

Best Team Name: Meat Cups
2017
(Holy Changes Batman!)
Going into the 2017 season the league knew that some things just had to change. The days of putting up with dead-weight teams needed to end, and unbalanced schedules needed to be addressed as well, amongst other things. The commish called for the first ever "Summer meetings" to discuss and vote on possible rule changes. For those that showed, some interesting topics were debated and a multitude of changes swept into the league. We first saw the addition of Tyler and Cerca into the league as the first change. Then defensive scoring was updated that saw the greatest pushback this league may ever see! Conferences were split into THREE for the first time as well as a wild-card format that looked to solve any bad-luck scheduling. Talk about a period of adjustment... this was one. But the league pulled through it! We had the most successful season to date as a record setting number of trades occured (along with the new non-snake draft picks the following season), our first-ever "Pick your opponent" playoff system piqued curiosity, and a late keeper declaration allowed a little bit of breathing room for all during the preseason. Tony wound up with his first championship since 2010 in the end and a new more open league changes voting system was developed for the following season.

Best Team Name: I Touchdown There
2018
(Veni vidi - VETO!)
We welcomed in yet another new member as the previous year's "Dead Team" rule took effect. Sean Leary joined the league as it's 27th overall member replacing veteran Sean Kenney. New rule changes took effect as a modified defensive scoring system was implimented as well as an offensive scoring adjustment that reduced the importance of the TD as many members felt it was too heavily skewed to. Tony's team rampaged through the schedule setting a record for points, but was cut down by a red-hot "Kupp Check" team in the Championship. This season may best be remembered though for an insane number of childish vetos during the most critical time of the year, the trade deadline. Fair trades were shot down, lopsided trades were let through- the wild west didn't have anything on this league- which was a shame! Changes are coming to rectify that though. The league saw long time member Chris Martin bid adieu.

Best Team Name: Off Constantly
2019
(Only as Good as Your Options)
After the debacle of the previous year's veto-frenzy, the NJCO tension peaked. After a close league wide vote, it was determined that no longer would the fate of trades be in other owners hands. The days of the "instant trade" were born! The days of spite-vetos, "I could have offered better" vetoes, and just plain troll-vetoing came to an end. Owners were now free to make whatever moves they wanted as long as it wasn't a blatantly obvious collusive move. To also help out the bottom feeders of the leage, optional keepers became a thing. Owners could now decide to not keep anybody AND in the process trade 1st and 2nd round picks. This opened up a whole new world of offers being made, of which 20 were accepted!

The season itself saw the "To Be Named" conference dominate the regular season with 3 of its 4 teams finishing above .500 and also seeing the Champion come out of it as well (Tommy C).

Best Team Name: MPD Little Guy
2020
(Pandemic- Nuff Said)
Coronavirus! The worldwide pandemic of COVID-19 threatened if there would even be a 2020 season! There was, but it wasn't very normal to start. Outbreaks caused teams schedules to be mixed up, we had some players opt out, and somehow Tom Brady won another Super Bowl at the age of 100. On the NJCO front, everybody finally got a taste of what optional keepers was like as the draft caught quite a few of them off guard. Then MONSTER trades went down during the season as multiple 1st and 2nd round picks traded hands. The playoffs saw the move of the century by Tom Leon, benching Russell Wilson for Ryan-freakin'-Tannehill, which squeaked him past Tony and into the Championship vs the defending champ Tommy Cohen. Tommy C eventually did come out on top for the repeat in one of the most balanced years we've ever had. No team had double-digit wins or double-digit losses! We also saw the most trades executed in a single year with 21!

Best Team Name: MCMom+theCrispySocks
2021
(Back to Reality)
The year of Kupp - Cooper Kupp had an absolutely dominating season and the team named after him cruised to yet another Championship... despite not having him. Tommy had the benefit of drafting Jonathan Taylor and watching the Colts running back go crazy in the second half of the season- despite the Colts not making the playoffs. Just like the owner of Cooper Kupp, Tyler, somehow missed the playoffs as well! Tyler also had Deebo Samuel.. so the top two WRs, and yeah. Tony had a fortunate first three weeks and then traded away his entire 2022 draft again just to fall in the playoffs and only win "the hug".

Best Team Name: Uno Moss RBBC por favor
2022
(Almost Domination)
"I OWN YOU!" - Travis's team was putting up a season for the ages, blowing away just about everybody in his way with ease, and barely making a single move to his roster in the process! But then, in a blink of an eye, it all came crashing down. Team "Back in Black" had finally flipped the script and went from a perennial last place team to a dominant wrecking ball. A wrecking ball though that got dented by Sean Leary's "Uncle Dad" along the way. When playoff time came, Travis set out to avenge his one single loss all year only for Uncle Dad to go full "Aaron Rodgers vs the Bears" on him with yet ANOTHER painful loss, bouncing Travis to the consolation matchup in the process. Sean's team though would face a red hot Team Crawford in the Championship, who blitzed his way to a yet another title with a solid smackdown. Tom Leon also made the playoffs with his best team ever assembled, yet he fell short twice and wound up with only a hug himself. Greg became the only 4x NJCO Champion in the process!

Best Team Name: Preferred Customers
2023
(The F5 Tornado)
Cruise Control - In what may go down as the single greatest season in NJCO history, SOB's "Sandy Hookers" did something that's never been done before... defeat every single team in the league. SOB claimed his third Championship with a final record of 15-1, avenging that only loss with a victory over Cerca in the first round of the playoffs. We saw the entire Rispoli divison dominate the OOC schedule creating yet another scenario where a 10-4 Tyler missed the playoffs. Tommy Cohen made his way back to the Championship game but was ultimately disappointed as Stefon Diggs became contagious with poor point totals and he got to the whole team. And to close, we finally saw somethign we ain't never seen before- Tony actually trading FOR draft picks (but it was really weird because he totally could have made a run at the Wild Card so... shrug?).
